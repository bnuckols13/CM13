Clinical Summary & Treatment Plan
Patient: Charity | Clinician: Brian | Date: October 2025

Core Understanding: The Unified Challenge

Central Clinical Insight: Charity's eating disorder IS her neurodivergence manifesting in adult life. This is not two separate problems—it's one unified challenge.
When did this emerge? The eating disorder appeared when three critical factors converged:
1. Protective factors disappeared
Parents stopped managing meals
↓
2. Life got more complex
Adult responsibilities exceeded childhood coping capacity
↓
3. Old systems stopped working
Perfectionist organizing that saved her as a kid now paralyzes her
↓
EATING DISORDER EMERGENCE
The Central Tension: The same brilliant, intense, absorptive mind that makes Charity an exceptional poet makes routine eating nearly impossible.

Main Issue: What's Actually Happening With Food

1.1: The Perfectionism Trap

Wants meals to be meaningful, cultural, "done right"
(the "ten-meal")
↓
Executing this requires
OVERWHELMING EXECUTIVE FUNCTION
↓
Falls back to childhood survival foods
(frozen, canned, boxed)
↓
These feel chronically unsatisfying
↓
AVOIDANCE ENTIRELY
The perfectionism trap: High standards create paralysis, leading to complete avoidance

1.2: The Reward Problem

✓ Activities That Work
Creative work → immediate emotional feedback
Work tasks → visible progress
Clear reward loops = engagement possible
✗ Why Food Doesn't Work
Food → nothing
Just incomplete loops
Plus sensory overwhelm
= No dopamine reward = No motivation

1.3: The Sensory Overwhelm

Food sensations can be overstimulating (textures, tastes, smells)
Current Coping Mechanism: Uses cannabis/alcohol to numb sensory experience enough to eat
This creates a concerning dependency pattern
BUT: It's currently her only access point to hunger/eating
Clinical Note: This isn't "substance abuse" in the traditional sense—it's adaptive self-medication for neurodivergent sensory overwhelm. However, it's unsustainable and needs replacement with healthier accommodation strategies.

1.4: The Executive Function Cascade

Every Single Meal Requires:
Planning what to eat
+
Shopping for ingredients
+
Time management
+
Cooking execution
+
Cleanup afterwards
×
PERFECTIONISM
applied to each step
=
COMPLETE PARALYSIS

Deeper Layer: The Grief Beneath the Symptoms

"I remember how it felt to be younger and to feel like I had things under control...I'm so disconnected from that."
Charity is mourning something profound: the loss of her identity as a competent person
What Worked in Childhood: Elaborate compensatory systems that gave her control and safety
Color-coded clothes to simplify morning decisions
Ritualistic routines that created predictability
Constant reorganizing to maintain environmental control
These systems compensated beautifully for ADHD symptoms
They prevented punishment and created safety
They worked brilliantly
"The things that made me strong are the things that are holding me back now."
The Paradox: The perfectionist systems that protected her as a child now create paralysis as an adult. She's not failing—her old tools are mismatched to adult complexity.
This isn't just about food. It's about:
Losing her identity as someone competent
Starting over at "ground zero"
Not knowing how to build a life that fits her actual brain
Grieving the person she thought she would become

Treatment Barrier: Why Previous Care Has Been Frustrating

PROVIDERS SEE:
Articulate & Insightful
Patient
⟶
PROVIDERS THINK:
"You should be able to
figure this out.
Use your strengths!"
The Fatal Misunderstanding: Providers don't understand that her "strengths" (perfectionism, complex systems) are precisely what's blocking her from eating.
What She Actually Needs: The opposite approach—radical simplicity, externalized executive function, and permission to be "wrong."

Treatment Framework: One Clear Path Forward

Core Principle

Stop trying to make Charity's brain do what it can't.
Engineer her environment instead.
OLD APPROACH:
"Fix" the brain
through willpower
⟶
NEW APPROACH:
Design environment
to support brain
Shift from internal change to external accommodation

The Single Most Important Goal

Establish consistent nutrition through ANY means necessary,
abandoning all perfectionism
What This Means Practically:

✓ Meal replacement shakes = VALID MEAL
✓ Protein bar + apple = VALID MEAL
✓ Bowl of cereal = VALID MEAL
✓ Same meal every day = VALID APPROACH
✓ "Boring" food = PERFECTLY ACCEPTABLE

The goal is nutrition entering the body, not culinary achievement.
Treatment Philosophy: We are not teaching Charity to overcome her neurodivergence. We are building a sustainable eating system that works with her brain, not against it.
Next Steps:

→ Identify 3-5 "minimum viable meals" that require zero executive function
→ Remove all food-related decision-making from Charity's plate
→ Externalize meal timing through alarms/reminders
→ Address sensory overwhelm with occupational therapy consultation
→ Begin grief work around loss of childhood competence identity
